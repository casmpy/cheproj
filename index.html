Introduction


Water wastage is a significant environmental concern that affects our planet's sustainability. The world's population is expected to reach 9.7 billion by 2050, and with the increasing demand for water from various sectors such as agriculture, industry, and households, water wastage has become a pressing issue. According to the United Nations, over two billion people live in countries experiencing high water stress, and this number is expected to increase to over four billion by 2050. Water is a finite resource, and its availability and quality are essential for human health and wellbeing, as well as for the environment. Wastage of water refers to the unnecessary use or excessive consumption of water that leads to its depletion, resulting in severe environmental and economic consequences. Every drop of water that is wasted has a ripple effect on the ecosystem, and the impact is felt by both humans and wildlife

Causes & Effects


Water shortage is a pressing issue that affects many parts of the world. It is caused by a combination of natural phenomena, human activities, and climate change. The effects of water shortage can be severe and far-reaching, impacting public health, food security, economic development, ecosystems, and social stability. One of the main causes of water shortage is climate change, which has led to a decrease in rainfall and an increase in extreme weather events such as droughts and floods. The growing world population and increasing demand for water is another cause, putting pressure on water resources. Over-extraction of groundwater, pollution, and poor water management practices also contribute to water scarcity. The effects of water shortage are many and varied. Public health is impacted by a lack of access to clean and safe water, leading to the spread of waterborne diseases such as cholera, typhoid, and diarrhea. Food security is another concern, with water shortage leading to crop failure and food insecurity, particularly in developing countries. Water scarcity can also impact economic development, limiting industrial growth, reducing tourism, and hindering job creation.



Solutions


  • Fix leaks: Leaks are a major cause of water wastage. Fixing leaks in pipes, faucets, and toilets can help save a significant amount of water.
  • Use efficient appliances: Choosing water-efficient appliances like dishwashers, washing machines, and showerheads can reduce water usage.
  • Adopt water-saving habits: Simple habits like turning off the tap while brushing your teeth, taking shorter showers, and watering plants during cooler hours of the day can save a lot of water.
  • Harvest rainwater: Collecting and storing rainwater for household use is an effective way to reduce dependence on freshwater sources.
  • Use recycled water: Recycled water, also known as greywater, can be used for activities like watering plants, flushing toilets, and cleaning, reducing the need for freshwater.

Jal Jeevan Mission
Launched in 2019

Jal Jeevan Mission aims to provide piped drinking water to every rural household in India by 2024. The program focuses on water conservation and the rejuvenation of water sources, with an emphasis on community participation.


Atal Bhujal Yojana
Launched in 2020

Atal Bhujal Yojana aims to improve groundwater management in the country. The program focuses on the sustainable use of groundwater resources, with an emphasis on community-based management.


National Water Policy
Backed By Government

The National Water Policy is a comprehensive framework that guides the management of water resources in the country. The policy promotes water conservation, the efficient use of water, and the integration of water management with other sectors like agriculture and industry.
